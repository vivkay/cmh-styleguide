Introduction

Our websites are globally connected platforms that showcase the extraordinary events, objects, people and stories that are presented by our world-class institutions.

Principles

Goals

We aim to serve and delight our diverse online audiences by presenting our expansive and richly varied content in an accessible, flexible and compelling way.

Principles

The CMH and CWM websites use the same framework. This website design should be informative, easy to use, and fun to explore.

Informative

The website must support a range of graphical, textual and multimedia formats, to provide a range of information that is accessible, accurate and up-to-date.

Easy to Use

The user’s ability to navigate the wealth of information on our websites is facilitated through visual simplification and strategic placement of content, as well as:

  • A clear visual hierarchy of information.
  • A generous use of white space, to enhance readability.
  • Relevant and concise text.
  • Easily identifiable navigational links.
  • Content filters that enable user-centered content customization.

Fun to Explore

In this new Experience Economy, users are looking for ways to engage with content. Our website will tell a rich and engaging story of our museums through:

  • Gorgeous photography.
  • Dynamic elements that respond to user interaction.
  • Fresh content that integrates live social media updates.

Style

To remain consistent with the museum brand guidelines, the core principles of flat design–the use of solid colour fills and clean lines–are to be applied throughout the site. Specific treatments, such as gradients and drop-shadows, are not consistent with our branding guidelines and are therefore to be avoided.

  • The Canadian War Museum green has been slightly muted as the original mix appears fluorescent in a digital format.

Typography

Thoughtful typography is critical to creating an accessible and enjoyable user-experience. Key components of typography–hierarchy, contrast, legibility, leading (line-height), contrast, and alignment– are critical in the organization, presentation and prioritization of information.

Digital Fonts

The web fonts for the CMH/CWM brand are Futura (web version) and Arial (web version). These clean, crisp sans-serif fonts offer flexibility through various available web-sizes, and readability.
